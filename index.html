Miami Race Week Begins — iRacing Week 7 Live, F1 Sprint Format Friday-Sunday, Antonelli Leads

Race week is on. The F1 Miami Grand Prix (1–3 May) is the season resumption after a five-week gap caused by the Bahrain and Saudi cancellations — treat it like a second season opener. IndyCar — Long Beach

Palou Wins Acura Grand Prix of Long Beach — First Long Beach Win, 22nd Career Victory

Alex Palou (#10 Chip Ganassi Racing Honda) took his first-ever win at the streets of Long Beach on Sunday, after pole-sitter Felix Rosenqvist (#60 Meyer Shank) lost the lead during the final round of green-flag pit stops and finished second, +3.97s back. Scott Dixon rounded out the podium ahead of Kyle Kirkwood and Pato O'Ward. With three wins in the opening six rounds of 2026, Palou reclaimed the championship lead, +17 over Kirkwood. Round 7: Indianapolis Road Course (May 9), then the Indy 500 (May 24).

DTM Race 2 — Result

Engel Wins DTM Race 2 at Red Bull Ring — Wittmann 2nd, Auer 3rd

Pole-sitter Maro Engel (Winward Mercedes-AMG) bounced back from his Saturday pit-stop drama to win DTM Race 2 on Sunday at the Red Bull Ring. Marco Wittmann (Schubert Motorsport BMW M4 GT3 Evo) crossed the line second, with Lucas Auer (Mercedes-AMG) third — an all-Mercedes-and-BMW podium. Combined with Thomas Preining's Race 1 victory the day before, the championship leaves Spielberg effectively level: Preining and Engel tied at the top, Auer the only other consistent podium-grabber. Next round: Zandvoort, 16–17 May.

Thomas' Pick — Hands-On

Sim-Lab DDX TorqueSync — I Drove Both at the SimRacing Expo Dortmund

Sim-Lab is moving from rigs and pedals into the wheelbase game with the DDX26 (26 Nm) and DDX39 (39 Nm) TorqueSync — and I got to drive both of them back-to-back at the SimRacing Expo in Dortmund. The FFB is razor-sharp and noticeably faster to communicate kerbs and grip loss than anything else I tried at the show.
