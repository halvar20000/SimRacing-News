🏃 About the CAS Community

Where passion meets technology — a home for iRacing enthusiasts

Community

Welcome to CAS

The CAS-iRacing Community is a welcoming place for sim racers of all skill levels. Whether you're a newcomer just getting started in iRacing or an experienced racer, CAS offers a supportive environment to improve your driving and connect with like-minded enthusiasts.

cas-community.com
Philosophy

Inclusive & Respectful Racing

CAS values an open, non-elitist atmosphere. The community focuses on mutual support, fair racing, and helping weaker drivers improve. Aggressive and disrespectful behavior is not tolerated — clean racing and sportsmanship come first.

Core Values
Founder

Founded by Andreas Wuschnakowski

Active in sim racing since 2021, Andreas founded CAS out of a desire to create a better community experience for newcomers. His own early experiences inspired the community's welcoming and supportive approach.

🏁 Leagues & Series

Well-organized leagues and individual events — close to real motorsport

League

GT3 – WCT (World Championship Tour)

The flagship GT3 series of the CAS community. Race the world's best GT3 machinery in a professionally organized championship that aims to replicate the excitement of real-world GT3 racing.

Details
League

IEC – International Endurance Championship

Endurance racing at its finest. Team up with fellow community members for longer races that test consistency, strategy, and driver management. A true test of teamwork and patience.

New for next season: The IEC will use iRaceControl for professional-grade race management — live tracking, automated stewarding, incident logging, and PDF race reports.

Details
League

Combined Cup Paused

A rotating single-class format — each week a completely different car. The current season cycles through three totally different machines: the Radical, the Ray FF1600 and a TCR touring car. That mix of philosophies — high-downforce prototype, lightweight open-wheeler, and front-drive tin-top — makes the Combined Cup one of the most demanding series in the community: braking points, balance and driving style must be re-learned week after week. Not a multi-class race.

Season paused: After Round 4, the 10th season of the Combined Cup has been paused due to insufficient participation from registered members. The R1–R4 results remain on record below. A return in a future season is possible — watch the CAS community channels for updates.

Details

SFL Cup

CAS GT3 WCT Season 12 · Round 4 · Spa-Francorchamps · 14 April 2026

🥉 Fritz Morawitz · overall P12

New entrant Mario Servan (AM pole) reclassified to Pro from Mugello onward.

Race Highlight

⏱️ The 1.5-Second Pit Stop That Won the Race

Luca Stein pitted first with a 41.9 s stop. Wonnenberg came in the following lap and delivered a 40.5 s stop — 1.5 s faster — emerging with the gap extended from under 1 s to 3.3 s. That margin essentially decided the race; Luca couldn't close it again.

Fight of the day: Stanzel vs Richter for P3 — Porsche traction out of La Source held off the last-lap Bus Stop attack.

📋 Full race report

Qualifying

Split format: 15 minutes AM, then 15 minutes Pro.

In AM, new entrant Mario Servan set the fastest AM time (2:16.692) but was pace-flagged for Pro reclassification — confirmed after the race. Actual AM order was led by Christoph Kiesel / Klaus Oberländer / Dirk Bolte in the 2:16s.

In Pro, Speed Monkeys dominated the timesheet. Pole: Yannick Wonnenberg (Lamborghini) — 2:15.324. P2 Luca Stein (AMG), P3 Mike Zocca (Porsche), P4 Dennis Ulli Richter.

Race

Clean start from Yannick into La Source. He and Luca built a ~5 s gap over the pack in the first stint. Behind them it was chaotic.

Key incidents:

  • Karl-Sören Schober tapped from behind in a three-way battle, spun at speed, lost the rear wing, crashed heavily
  • Mike Zocca lost the Porsche at high speed, fell from front-runner to P20+
  • Christoph Kiesel meatball-flagged after contact with Michael Krieger; Krieger got a drive-through for the same incident
  • Robert Zellner went airborne over a kerb at Raidillon and retired
  • Manfred Bahr, Ralf Mielke, Dani Platzer and Julian Borowski parked early

Pit window decided the win: Luca pitted first (41.9 s), Yannick one lap later with a 40.5 s stop, emerging 3.3 s clear.

Fight of the day: Henrik Stanzel (Neon Sim Sports Yellow Porsche) vs Dennis Ulli Richter for P3. Dennis closed to 0.2–0.4 s through the middle sector multiple times, but Porsche traction out of La Source kept reopening the gap on Kemmel. A final move into the Bus Stop on the last lap — not enough. Heroic defense.

Post-Race Interviews

Yannick Wonnenberg: expected harder slipstream pressure on Kemmel, but the Lambo was strong on straights and the AMG couldn't pull him in. The one-lap-longer stint plus a faster stop gave him the breathing room to drive it home controlled.

Dennis Ulli Richter: "Spa isn't really my track." Apologized on stream for the Karl-Sören contact — wasn't intentional. After the pit stop he couldn't hold Henrik; the Porsche had the better traction out of La Source every lap. Took P4 cleanly.

Off-Track Notes

  • Post-race briefing initiative: Dennis & Yannick (Speed Monkeys) launched a Wednesday post-race review format — casual, community-focused discussion of incidents the day after each race.
  • SimRacing Hub plug on stream: Andreas recommended simracing-hub.com live as a "super great new site covering all sim racing updates, updated daily" — to be linked from the CAS homepage soon.
  • Next race: Mugello GP, Tuesday 21 April 2026 — Round 5, the half-distance point of the season.

Summary based on the full cas_sim_tv broadcast by Andreas.
