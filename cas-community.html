Community

Welcome to CAS

The CAS-iRacing Community is a welcoming place for sim racers of all skill levels. Whether you're a newcomer just getting started in iRacing or an experienced racer, CAS offers a supportive environment to improve your driving and connect with like-minded enthusiasts.

cas-community.com
Philosophy

Inclusive & Respectful Racing

CAS values an open, non-elitist atmosphere. The community focuses on mutual support, fair racing, and helping weaker drivers improve. Aggressive and disrespectful behavior is not tolerated — clean racing and sportsmanship come first.

Core Values
Founder

Founded by Andreas Wuschnakowski

Active in sim racing since 2021, Andreas founded CAS out of a desire to create a better community experience for newcomers. His own early experiences inspired the community's welcoming and supportive approach.

League

Combined Cup Paused

A rotating single-class format — each week a completely different car. The current season cycles through three totally different machines: the Radical, the Ray FF1600 and a TCR touring car. That mix of philosophies — high-downforce prototype, lightweight open-wheeler, and front-drive tin-top — makes the Combined Cup one of the most demanding series in the community: braking points, balance and driving style must be re-learned week after week. Not a multi-class race.

Season paused: After Round 4, the 10th season of the Combined Cup has been paused due to insufficient participation from registered members. The R1–R4 results remain on record below. A return in a future season is possible — watch the CAS community channels for updates.

CAS GT3 WCT Season 12 · Round 6 · Fuji International Speedway · 28 April 2026 · second half of the season begins

Race Highlight

🔥 Maurice Becker untouchable

The MG Down Force Porsche dominated Fuji from start to finish. Maurice Becker passed early leader Mario Severn cleanly, set the fastest lap of the race, and pulled away to a 13-second victory. Pole-sitter Severn held P2; championship contender Yannick Wonnenberg salvaged P3 in the Lamborghini, which struggled on the long Fuji straights.

Quote of the day — Becker: “The only person who could have beaten me was myself.”

📋 Full race report

Setting

Fuji International Speedway (FSW), 4.563 km, 16 turns, Grand Prix variant. Owned by Toyota, originally planned as a banked oval and now home of the WEC’s 6 Hours of Fuji. The 1.5 km start-finish straight is one of the longest in motorsport — setup is all about minimum drag. 32 starters on the grid in dry, moderate conditions: ambient 22°C, track around 25–27°C.

Qualifying

Split format as always: 15 minutes AM, then 15 minutes Pro.

In AM, pole went to Michael Endres (Geunit, Mustang) in 1:38.984. P2 Michael Krieger (Hafenegger, Porsche, +0.0xx), P3 Alexander Thieme (Dan Küchen, BMW M4) at 1:40.042, P4 Fritz Morawetz (NEON Red, Mustang), P5 Benjamin Warnow (NEON Yellow, Audi R8 — the only Audi in the field).

In Pro, Mario Severn (Porsche) snatched pole. Maurice Becker (MG Down Force, Porsche) lined up P2 within a tenth, with Karl-Sören Schober, Hendrik Stanzel and Yannick Wonnenberg filling out the top six. Dennis Ulli Richter never quite found his rhythm and qualified outside the top ten — uncharacteristic for him.

Race — first stint

The standing start was clean at the front but messy further back: five or six cars ran wide into the Daichi corner. Severn led from pole; Maurice Becker held P2 with an 0.8-second deficit and immediately settled into Severn’s tow on the long straight. Within a handful of laps Becker had the Porsche-on-Porsche pace advantage and took the lead, then started to pull away — visibly faster than anyone else on the day.

Lap 1 incident: Michael Krieger (P2 AM grid) was punted in a touch-up with returning entrant Florian Aigner and dropped to P16. He fought back through the field for the rest of the race and ultimately took the AM win.

Yannick Wonnenberg, the championship contender, struggled to extract pace from the Lamborghini on Fuji’s long straights and settled into a defensive third-to-fifth zone, taking until well into the second stint to consolidate P3.

Pit phase & second stint

Mandatory pit stop window opened just past half-distance. Cool track temps meant tyre wear was a non-issue: most front-runners stopped in 47–50 seconds with fuel only, no rubber change. Maurice Becker stopped on lap 18 (47.3 s); Severn stopped a lap later (50 s); they emerged in the same order with Becker now in the lead and a healthy gap.

From there Becker reeled off a string of mid-1:38s, including the race’s overall fastest lap (1:38.223), and disappeared up the road. Yannick Wonnenberg gradually closed on Severn but never quite found a clean overtaking opportunity on the straight.

The mid-pack three-way: the most entertaining battle of the race was for P4–P6 between Lukas Zörlaut (NEON Blue AMG), Luca-Maximilian Stein (Nova Nitro AMG) and Hendrik Stanzel (NEON Yellow Porsche). Stanzel finally cleared both Mercedes through the Coca-Cola Curves, briefly grabbed P4 — before contact with Stein shuffled positions again. Stein picked up a drive-through penalty and dropped to P10. Final order: Zörlaut P4, Stanzel P5, Micewski P6.

Notable incidents

  • Andi Weber got airborne over the notorious yellow exit kerbs at the Karussell, rolled the car — one of the most spectacular incidents of the season so far.
  • Antonio Cursio (CAS Tech Performance, McLaren) lost the rear braking into a slow corner and hit the wall — same setup-related issue we saw at Mugello.
  • Max Coldron (AM championship leader) had a bad day, picked up a meatball flag and never recovered — a rare off-day for the NEON Red driver.
  • Benjamin Schlosser, Holger Meissner, Christoph Kiesel all had lone spins or chicane misses that cost time without causing race-changing damage.

Final classification

Pro top 10: Becker, Severn, Wonnenberg, Zörlaut, Stanzel, Micewski, Marius Becker (Speed Monkeys, different driver), Krieger (best AM), Dennis Ulli Richter, Stein.

AM podium: Krieger, Thieme, Morawetz.

Off-track notes

  • iRaceControl Stage 1 went live for blue-flag enforcement (whisper messages at 0.5 s threshold). Stage 2 (automatic penalty) still pending the WCT driver vote.
  • SimRacing Hub plug: commentator Andreas cross-promoted this site live on stream as the place where race reports and post-race briefings are published — thanks Andreas.
  • New live race-charts overlay by Thomas Herbrig (sim_racing_broadcast) was tested during the broadcast — live standings worked, race-position charts still in development.
  • Florian Aigner (Phase 2 Motorsports) returned to the WCT grid; Thomas Kübler sat this one out.
  • Next WCT race: Brands Hatch GP (UK), Tuesday 5 May 2026 — Round 7 of 12.

Post-race interviews

Mario Severn (P2): “The Porsche has always been strong at Fuji, and with this week’s BoP we had a small extra advantage. Pace-wise it was always tight with Maurice in qualifying — in the race he was just on another level.”

Maurice Becker (P1): “The track is just incredible. Looking at the qualifying gap to Mario I thought it could be tense — in the race it was actually quite relaxed for me. The Porsche worked perfectly here, the Lambos and Mercedes really struggled. The only person who could have beaten me was myself.”

Championship

🏅️ Silvio doubles up — lead grows

Before Monza, Silvio led the standings on 228 points, André Rajkovic on 199. After sweeping both races at Monza, Silvio extends that cushion further with only four rounds to go. Fastest lap of the day: Willi Brandt, 1:47.1 — with Silvio and Rajkovic within a tenth of each other on pure pace.

Next round: Barcelona GP, Wednesday 7 May 2026

📋 Full race report

Setting

Autodromo Nazionale di Monza — the 1922 original, the Temple of Speed. Low-wing setups, huge slipstream on the straights, but three demanding chicanes that punish the smallest mistake. 12 drivers on the grid, clear weather, no rain in the forecast.

Qualifying

10-minute session, 3 timed laps. Early benchmark from Silvio (1:47.7), before Willi Brandt briefly took top spot with 1:47.6 — then ran wide and lost the lap. In the closing minutes André Rajkovic laid down a cleaner second run to take pole. Starting grid: Rajkovic, Silvio, Brandt, Klaus Oberländer, Benjamin Schlosser, Andy Weber, Thomas Felix, Christian Schlosser, Olli, Sean Pfennig, Jan Tobias Schrader, Ricardo Cavoto. Two-second spread from pole to P12 — a tight field.

Race 1 — 25 minutes, standard grid

Clean standing start — all 12 cars through the first two chicanes unscathed, a welcome change after the GT3 WCT chaos 48 hours earlier. The front four (Rajkovic, Silvio, Brandt, Oberländer) ran inside one second of each other for most of the first half. Sean Pfennig lost the tow early and dropped out of the lead group; a solo spin elsewhere in the pack claimed an early retirement.

The race-defining moment came in a three-wide battle into the second chicane. Silvio, Rajkovic and Willi Brandt were side-by-side entering the braking zone — Brandt’s inside dive connected lightly with Rajkovic, who spun. Broadcaster’s read: “unintentional, but a clear racing incident.” Silvio escaped with a clean run out of the chicane and a 3.7 s gap; Rajkovic dropped to P6. Brandt was arguably punished more by the consequences than Rajkovic — he lost time too and fell behind Andy Weber.

From there Silvio managed the race from the front. Benjamin Schlosser capitalised to grab P2, with Rajkovic fighting back to P3 by the end. Brandt recovered to P5 after a late pass on Oberländer into the Parabolica.

Race 1 result: Silvio, Schlosser, Rajkovic, Oberländer, Brandt, Weber, Pfennig, Christian Schlosser, Olli, Felix, Schrader, Cavoto.

Race 2 — 25 minutes, reverse-grid top 8

With Ricardo Cavoto (P12 in R1) lining up on pole and the quick drivers further back, Monza’s long straights teed up exactly the kind of charging race the reverse-grid format is built for. Clean through the first chicane again — another strong start from a very disciplined field.

Jan Tobias Schrader took the lead from Cavoto. Silvio, Brandt and Rajkovic began carving through the midfield on the power sections. Rajkovic worked his way into the lead over the first ten minutes, before the unstoppable Silvio caught up and retook P1 with a slipstream pass on the start-finish straight.

The single big incident of Race 2 was at the Parabolica, where Andy Weber and Olli Utz went side-by-side on entry. Weber moved across without seeing Olli on the inside — contact, both cars compromised. Broadcaster’s read (not a stewards’ call): fault with Weber, most likely a spotter gap rather than intent. Olli still recovered to P7; Weber dropped to P10.

A brief internet dropout on the broadcaster’s end (Thomas streaming from France) caused a short stream outage, during which Schrader passed Brandt for track position. The stream came back in time for the final fight: Silvio clear of Rajkovic by about a second, Brandt reeling Schrader in but running out of laps.

Race 2 result: Silvio, Rajkovic, Brandt, Schrader, Cavoto, Oberländer, Olli, Benjamin Schlosser, Christian Schlosser, Weber, Pfennig, Felix.

Notes from the booth

  • Fastest lap overall: Willi Brandt, 1:47.1, with Silvio (1:47.2) and Rajkovic (1:47.3) a breath behind — the top three were separated by two tenths on pure pace.
  • Monza rewarded clean sectors over risk. Several close overtakes were called off because the front driver held a good line — the speaker’s observation that Monza “looks easy to pass on paper, but if the lead car runs clean you barely get by.”
  • Trophies for the full season have been ordered by organiser Andreas — they’re on their way.
  • Next round: Barcelona GP, Wednesday 7 May 2026. Then Silverstone GP, Hockenheim GP, and Okayama (Full Course) to close the season.

📝 Season Finale Round 8 · 🇺🇸 Road Atlanta · 27 April 2026 · 8 drivers · 45-minute race
Race Podium

🏆 Venanzi, Eckert, Beer

🥇 Pitt Venanzi — Heavy Metal Racing
🥈 Philipp Eckert — Firefly E-Racing (running as Heavy Metal)
🥉 Matthias Beer — Melanani Racing

Pole: Beer 1:26.x · 53°C track temp — a true tyre-management race

2026 Champion

🏅️ Matthias Beer — new TSS GT4 Champion

Beer seals Season 3 with a controlled P3 finish, dethroning defending champion Pitt Venanzi. The two had run nose-to-tail at the front for most of the race; in the closing minutes Beer backed out of the fight to bank the title without risk — even letting Eckert through for P2 on track.

Final championship: Beer — Venanzi — Eckert — Raab

Strategy of the Day

🔥 Eckert’s half-set tyre gamble

With the track at 53°C and rubber dying lap by lap, Philipp Eckert made a last-minute call in the pits to change only the left-side tyres — front-left and rear-left. The longer stop dropped him on track, but in the second half he ran 1:26 lap times nobody else could match, ate up the gap and overtook Raab into the chicane to claim P2.

Venanzi’s win came on full fuel-saving discipline — he crossed the line with just 0.1 litre left.

📋 Full race report

Setting

Road Atlanta — 4.06 km, fast and undulating. Air temperature 22°C, but a track that climbed past 53°C by lights-out turned the entire race into a tyre-and-fuel management exercise — the cars “driving like on soap,” in the broadcaster’s words. Eight drivers on the grid; many regulars sat the finale out once the championship picture was clear.

Pre-race notes

  • Kevin Hilkenhöfel removed from the league after the unsporting incident with Pitt Venanzi at the previous round; matter is closed.
  • Marcel (MarcellyanX commentator) announced this would be his last commentary for several weeks — he sets out on 12 May for a 3,500 km bicycle ride to North Cape (estimated 8–10 weeks). Andreas will cover commentary in the meantime.

Qualifying

10-minute Lone Quali. Late drama: Jürgen Raab held provisional pole through most of the session and looked set to take it — until Matthias Beer delivered a final-lap masterclass (mirroring exactly what he did at the previous round) to snatch P1 by 0.066 s. Starting grid: Beer, Raab, Eckert, Venanzi, Kübler, Olli Utz, Cavoto, Felix.

Olli Utz had a technical issue and started from the pit lane.

Race — first stint

At lights-out, Venanzi made the move of the day on the run to Turn 1: he passed both Raab and Eckert in one go, jumping from P4 to P2 behind Beer. Light contact between Raab and Eckert in the squeeze, but both kept going.

Olli Utz lost the rear coming out of the pits and crashed heavily into the barrier — race over for him on the spot, although he later returned to record a finishing position.

The lead trio — Beer, Venanzi, Eckert — settled into a tight group, with Venanzi sitting deliberately in Beer’s tow, saving fuel and tyres rather than attacking. Raab dropped out of slipstream range to P4 within ten minutes. Ricardo Cavoto kept understeering off-track on dying tyres and lost time with multiple slow-down zones; Thomas Kübler eventually had a 1:10-minute pit stop and dropped to the back.

The pit phase — tactics decide the race

  • Jürgen Raab pitted first, attempting an undercut on Eckert. It worked — he stopped 1.7 s shorter and emerged ahead of Eckert on track for P3.
  • Eckert took the gamble that defined the race: change only the left-side tyres (front and rear). Longer stop, but the new rubber would prove decisive.
  • Beer and Venanzi pitted together in the same lap. Neither changed tyres. Venanzi spent slightly less time stationary — thanks to his fuel-saving first stint — and emerged in P1, swapping the lead with Beer.

Race — second stint

The strategy unwound on track. Eckert, on his fresh left-sides, started clocking 1:26 lap times nobody else could match, devouring the gap to Raab. Caught him a couple of laps later, dived inside into the chicane, ran side-by-side through the next right and was through for P3.

Raab’s race then turned bad: a self-inflicted crash into the wall earned him a meatball flag and dropped him out of the leading group. He recovered to finish P4.

Eckert kept charging towards the leaders, turning the front fight into a three-way scrap. With three minutes left, Beer — the new champion-elect — visibly backed out: a fuel-saving lap that may also have been a deliberate decision to avoid risk. He let Eckert through for P2 and cruised home a champion in P3.

Final result & champion

P1 Venanzi, P2 Eckert, P3 Beer, P4 Raab, P5 Kübler, P6 Olli Utz, P7 Cavoto, P8 Felix.

2026 TSS GT4 Masters Champion: Matthias Beer (Melanani Racing). P2 in the championship: Pitt Venanzi (Heavy Metal Racing). P3: Philipp Eckert (Firefly E-Racing). P4: Jürgen Raab.

Voices from the post-race interviews

  • Pitt Venanzi: “The plan was always to sit behind Matthias and save fuel — if I’d gone with him I’d have killed the tyres in two laps. I came across the line with 0.1 l left. Almost didn’t make it.”
  • Philipp Eckert: “The race was brutal. I made a last-minute call to change only the left-side tyres in the stop. Second half I was running times I didn’t even believe I could hit at this track temperature. Would’ve loved to fight Matthias for the win, but he just slowed and waved me by — not sure why.”
  • Matthias Beer: backed off in the closing laps to bank the championship without risk — the two-tenths fuel-window-plus-no-incident strategy.
