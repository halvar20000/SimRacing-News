DTM 2026 Opens with a Split Verdict — Preining & Engel Share Spoils, Miami GP Up Next

The new DTM season is live and competitive. Thomas Preining (Manthey EMA Porsche) won Race 1 on Saturday at the Red Bull Ring after pole-sitter Maro Engel (Winward Mercedes) lost time on a sticky left-rear pit stop; Lucas Auer completed the podium and BMW debutant Jonas Buus stunned the paddock with P4. Engel hit back hard on Sunday: in Race 2 he won ahead of Marco Wittmann (BMW M4 GT3 Evo) and Auer, leaving the championship effectively tied at the top after one weekend. The focus now shifts to the F1 Miami Grand Prix (May 1-3, sprint format), with Kimi Antonelli leading the standings by nine points over George Russell. iRacing Week 7 kicks off tomorrow at the Tuesday reset, with GT3 moving to Brands Hatch GP across the anchor series.

Sim-Lab DDX TorqueSync — I Drove Both at the SimRacing Expo Dortmund

Sim-Lab is moving from rigs and pedals into the wheelbase game with the DDX26 (26 Nm) and DDX39 (39 Nm) TorqueSync — and I got to drive both of them back-to-back at the SimRacing Expo in Dortmund. The FFB is razor-sharp and noticeably faster to communicate kerbs and grip loss than anything else I tried at the show.

Patented TorqueSync™ control loop at 100 kHz, 350 V servo bus, 24-bit encoder, internal 1,200/1,500 W PSU, USB 3.0 passthrough, Quick-Release V2. Italian ISO manufacturing, TÜV SÜD approval, 4-year warranty. Pre-orders open; stock windows still tight.
